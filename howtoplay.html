Step-by-Step Guide to Playing D&D

1. Understand the Goal

D&D is a collaborative storytelling game where:

  • One player is the Dungeon Master (DM) (the narrator/world builder)
  • Everyone else plays characters in that world

You don’t “win” in the traditional sense—you create a story together, solve problems, and go on adventures.

2. Gather Your Group

You’ll need:

  • 1 Dungeon Master
  • 2–6 players (including you)

If no one wants to DM, you can try it yourself—it’s actually fun once you get the hang of it.

3. Get Basic Materials

At minimum:

  • Dice (especially a d20, the 20-sided die)
  • Character sheets
  • Pencils
  • The basic rules (free online)

Helpful but optional:

  • A rulebook (like the Player’s Handbook)
  • Miniatures or a map

4. Create a Character

Each player makes a character by choosing:

a. Race

Examples:

  • Human
  • Elf
  • Dwarf

b. Class (your role)

Examples:

  • Fighter (combat)
  • Wizard (magic)
  • Rogue (stealth)

c. Ability Scores

These determine how good you are at things:

  • Strength (physical power)
  • Dexterity (agility)
  • Constitution (health)
  • Intelligence (knowledge)
  • Wisdom (awareness)
  • Charisma (social skills)

d. Background & Personality

Give your character:

  • A backstory
  • Goals
  • Personality traits

This is what makes the game fun—your character feels like a real person.

5. The DM Prepares the Adventure

The DM:

  • Creates or uses a pre-made story
  • Describes the world
  • Controls enemies and NPCs (non-player characters)

Think of the DM like a game director + storyteller.

6. Start Playing (The Core Loop)

The game follows a simple pattern:

1. DM Describes the Scene

Example:

“You enter a dark cave. You hear something moving inside…”

2. Players Say What They Do

You respond:

“I draw my sword and move forward carefully.”

3. Roll Dice When Needed

If something is uncertain, you roll a d20:

  • High roll = success
  • Low roll = failure (or partial success)

The DM tells you what to roll and what happens.

7. Combat (When Fights Happen)

Combat is more structured:

Step-by-step:

  1. Roll initiative (determines turn order)
  2. Take turns:
    • Move
    • Take an action (attack, cast spell, etc.)
  3. Roll to hit:
    • d20 + modifiers
  4. Roll damage if you hit

Repeat until the fight ends.

8. Roleplay & Decision Making

Outside combat, you:

  • Talk to characters
  • Solve puzzles
  • Explore
  • Make choices that affect the story

There’s no single “right” way to play—be creative.

9. Level Up

As you play:

  • You gain experience (XP) or level up at story points
  • Your character gets stronger:
    • More health
    • New abilities
    • Better skills

10. Keep the Game Fun

Some unwritten rules:

  • Don’t interrupt others
  • Work as a team
  • Respect the DM’s rulings
  • Be creative and have fun
